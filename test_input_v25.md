
# The Physics of Roller Coasters

## Introduction to Energy
Energy is the property that must be transferred to an object in order to perform work on, or to heat, the object.

## Potential and Kinetic Energy
In a roller coaster, energy constantly transforms between potential and kinetic forms.
- **Potential Energy (PE)**: Stored energy based on height. $PE = mgh$.
- **Kinetic Energy (KE)**: Energy of motion. $KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$.

## Worked Example: Calculating Speed
**Problem**: A 500kg car drops from 20m. What is its speed at the bottom?
**Solution**:
1. At top: $PE = 500 \cdot 9.8 \cdot 20 = 98,000 J$. $KE = 0$.
2. At bottom: All PE becomes KE.
3. $98,000 = \frac{1}{2}(500)v^2$.
4. $196 = v^2 \rightarrow v = 14 m/s$.

## Quiz Time
1. What happens to PE as the car falls?
   a) Increases
   b) Decreases
   c) Stays same
   d) Becomes Zero
   **Answer**: b) Decreases (It converts to KE).

## Important Terms to Remember
- **Inertia**: Resistance to change in motion.
- **Friction**: Force opposing motion.
- **Gravity**: Force pulling down ($9.8 m/s^2$).
- **Velocity**: Speed with direction.
- **Conservation**: Energy is never lost.
